10 Must-Try Bangkok Street Foods and Where to Find Them

Bangkok is a street food paradise. From sizzling woks to smoky grills, the city's streets are packed with delicious bites. If you want to experience Thai food at its best, you have to try it on the streets. But with so many options, where do you start?

Some dishes stand out as must-tries. And knowing where to find the best versions makes all the difference. Here are 10 Bangkok street foods you shouldn't miss — plus where to get them.

1. Pad Thai (Stir-Fried Noodles)

Pad Thai is Thailand's most famous noodle dish. It's a mix of stir-fried rice noodles, shrimp or tofu, eggs, bean sprouts, and a tamarind-based sauce. The sauce gives it a perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and umami flavors.

Where to try: Thipsamai Pad Thai (Old Town). This legendary spot has been serving Pad Thai since 1966. Try their signature version wrapped in an egg omelet for extra flavor.

💡

Pro Tip: Ask for the "Superb Pad Thai" cooked with shrimp oil for a richer taste. Pair it with their fresh orange juice — it's the perfect combo!

2. Som Tam (Green Papaya Salad)

Som Tam is a refreshing yet fiery salad that balances sweet, sour, salty, and spicy in every bite. Made with shredded green papaya, tomatoes, long beans, peanuts, dried shrimp, and a dressing of lime juice, fish sauce, palm sugar, and chilies.

Where to try: Som Tam Jay So (Silom Soi 20). A no-frills stall that serves some of the most authentic Isaan-style som tam in Bangkok.

3. Mango Sticky Rice

This iconic Thai dessert is pure comfort. Sweet glutinous rice is steamed with coconut milk, then served with slices of perfectly ripe mango and a drizzle of coconut cream. The contrast of warm rice, cool mango, and rich coconut is heavenly.

Where to try: Mae Varee (Thong Lor BTS). Locals line up here daily for the freshest mango sticky rice in the city.

⏰

Timing tip: Arrive before 4pm — Mae Varee sells out fast, especially during mango season (March to June).

4. Guay Teow (Thai Noodle Soup)

Guay Teow is Thailand's everyday comfort food — a steaming bowl of noodles in a fragrant broth with your choice of meat. You pick your noodle type (thin rice, wide rice, egg, or glass noodles), your protein, and your toppings.

Where to try: Kuay Teow Reua (Victory Monument area). Known for boat noodles, these tiny bowls pack an intense dark broth flavour that keeps you ordering more.

5. Khao Man Gai (Chicken Rice)

Khao Man Gai is deceptively simple — poached chicken served over rice cooked in chicken fat, with a punchy ginger-chili dipping sauce. When done well, it's one of the most satisfying meals in Bangkok. The rice should be fragrant and slightly oily, the chicken tender and juicy.

Where to try: Kuang Heng (Pratunam). A legendary Pratunam institution that's been perfecting chicken rice for decades.
